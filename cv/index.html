Professional Summary

I work at the boundary between AI research and real-world enterprise deployment, most interested in what makes AI systems trustworthy in practice, not merely capable. I spent two decades taking emerging technology into regulated industries: a national EV-charging business built inside New Zealand’s utility sector, a governance training firm scaled to €5M ARR across three continents, and executive advisory at KPMG. Over the last two years I retooled hands-on, earning a First Class Honours Master of Artificial Intelligence. My research runs across deep learning and NLP to where models meet their limits: removing concepts from trained models without retraining them, and representing knowledge so that retrieval can be trusted. I build and run the systems myself, end-to-end, on open-source models. The discipline throughout is the same: evaluation, constraints, and controllability, in service of AI that is safe, well-governed, and genuinely beneficial when enterprises hand it real work.
